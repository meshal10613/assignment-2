Standard Curriculum

Kinder (3-6) Years

A kindergarten student is typically a child between the ages of 4 and 6 years old who is enrolled in kindergarten, which is often the first stage of formal education. Learning to share, cooperate, and communicate with peers and adults.

Elementary School

An elementary school is the foundational stage of formal education for children, typically covering grades 1 through 5 or 6 (ages 6 to 11 or 12, depending on the education system). Emphasis on deeper critical thinking and advanced academic content.

Middle (10-16) Years

Middle school is an educational stage that bridges elementary school and high school, typically covering grades 6 through 8 (ages 11 to 14). It plays a crucial role in students' academic, social, and emotional development during early adolescence.
